Some random information about water

Ocean water covers 71% of Earth and contains 3.5% salt, making it undrinkable. It regulates climate, supports marine life, and produces much of Earth's oxygen.

Drinking water must be free of contaminants like bacteria and heavy metals. It comes from various sources and is treated to ensure safety and proper pH balance.

Lake water can be freshwater or saline, with varying nutrient levels. It supports aquatic life but is vulnerable to pollution from runoff and waste.

River water flows from higher to lower elevations, shaping landscapes. It provides drinking water, irrigation, and energy but is sensitive to pollution.
